Project Overview

Environmental & Social Histories

This project examines the environmental and social histories of Chinese immigrants in California from the mid-19th century to the present. Using a Bay Area lens centered on Angel Island, San Francisco and Oakland Chinatowns, the Sacramento–San Joaquin Delta, and the Sierra Nevada corridors, the site traces:

  • Federal and state laws governing land, property, and mobility.
  • Labor that reshaped ecologies through mining, railroads, levee building, agriculture, and fisheries.
  • Urban resilience in Chinatowns amid environmental health threats and redevelopment.
  • Post-1965 activism around greenspace, environmental justice, and community stewardship.
